### Issues Found

1. **[MEDIUM] No Sitemap reference in robots.txt.** While a sitemap exists at `/sitemap_index.xml` (referenced in llms.txt), it is not declared in robots.txt. This is a missed signal for AI crawlers that use robots.txt as their entry point for content discovery.

2. **[LOW] Calendar path blocks are broad.** The wildcard pattern `Disallow: /*/action~*/` could theoretically match non-calendar paths if the site structure changes, though currently this appears safe.

3. **[HIGH] JavaScript-rendered content is invisible to crawlers.** The blog posts returned empty content bodies when fetched -- only JavaScript/CSS frameworks loaded. This means GPTBot, ClaudeBot, and other AI crawlers that do not execute JavaScript will see **no blog content at all**. This is the single biggest crawler access issue, even though robots.txt allows access. Allowing access to empty pages is worse than blocking them.

**Recommendation:** Add `Sitemap: https://kevgroup.com/sitemap_index.xml` to robots.txt. More critically, ensure blog content is server-side rendered so crawlers can access it without JavaScript execution.

## 6. Detailed Findings Summary

### What KEV Group Does Well

- **No AI crawler blocks.** All 12 major AI crawlers have full access. This is the correct approach for a company that benefits from AI visibility.
- **llms.txt exists and is auto-maintained.** Generated by Yoast SEO, it covers the major content sections and includes blog post excerpts with proprietary statistics.
- **Proprietary data assets.** The $49M fraud report, 62% cash skimming statistic, and 93 confirmed fraud cases are exactly the type of original data that AI models prefer to cite. This is a genuine competitive advantage.
- **FAQ schema on overview page.** The 4 FAQ pairs are well-structured and directly quotable.
- **Strong LinkedIn presence.** 38K+ followers and complete company profile provide a solid brand signal.
- **Structured data implementation.** Organization, Product, FAQ, Video, and BreadcrumbList schemas help AI models understand entity relationships.

### What Needs Improvement

- **JavaScript-rendered blog content is the critical gap.** KEV's most valuable content (fraud statistics, audit guides) lives in blog posts that return empty HTML to crawlers. This effectively nullifies the excellent citability of the underlying content.
- **Wikipedia absence is a major entity recognition gap.** Without a Wikipedia article, AI models may not recognize "KEV Group" as a notable entity, reducing citation likelihood.
- **Homepage is a citability dead zone.** The most prominent page on the site contains the least citable content -- generic marketing copy with zero statistics or quotable claims.
- **Review platform presence is invisible.** Despite claiming 4.8/5 from 1,000+ ratings, these are not visible on G2, Capterra, or Trustpilot, where AI models commonly source review data.
- **No llms-full.txt.** This is a missed opportunity to provide complete content to AI models, especially given the blog rendering issues.

### Competitive Context

In the K-12 school finance SaaS space, KEV Group has strong underlying content assets (proprietary fraud data, 30+ years of domain expertise, extensive case studies) but is undermined by technical delivery issues. A competitor with weaker content but proper SSR and a Wikipedia article would likely outperform KEV Group in AI search citations today. The good news is that the content quality is already there -- the priority actions are primarily about making that content accessible to AI systems.
